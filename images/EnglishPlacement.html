English Placement Test


The purpose of the writing portion of the English Placement Test (EPT) is to place students in appropriate English classes so that they can satisfy not only the University’s English composition requirement but so they can also be better prepared for their General Education and major courses.

WHO NEEDS TO TAKE THE ENGLISH PLACEMENT TEST?

The English Placement Test (EPT) is given to all incoming freshmen and to transfer students who have not successfully completed ENGL 101 or an equivalent course at their previous institution. The EPT is divided into two parts: a writing test and a reading test.

Not all students are required to take the reading portion of the EPT. Students who enter the university with an ACT reading score of 20 or higher, will be excused from taking the reading portion of the English Placement Test but will still need to take the writing portion.

Click reading or writing for more information about the test.

TEST REGISTRATION

Registration for the English Placement Test can be done in person at the Academic Advisement and Testing Center, located in Lech Walesa Hall, LWH 0027 or by phone at 773-442-5293. Refer to the current test schedule. Any cancellations or changes must be made 48 hours in advance. Absence from an exam without proper cancellation will prevent further registration; a student who fails to appear 3 times for his/her test will not be eligible for future registration of the test.

RESULTS AND SCORE INTERPRETATION

The results for the English Placement test are available approximately 2 weeks after the test. Students may obtain results from the Academic Advisement and Testing Center, located in Lech Walesa Hall, Room LWH 0027, upon presentation of a photo ID. You may call us at (773) 442-5293 to check if the results are in however, we cannot give results over the phone.

TEST START TIMES
Please note that placement tests are typically scheduled back to back on the same day beginning first with the Math Placement Test followed by the writing portion of the English Placement Test and finally, the reading portion of the English Placement Test. Therefore, if you are not scheduled to take the Math Placement Test,  please be aware that the writing portion of the English Placement Test begins 1 hour and 20 minutes after the start time indicated on the test schedule, and the reading portion begins 2 hours and 20 minutes after the time indicated on the schedule.
