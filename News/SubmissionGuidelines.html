Student Affairs E-Newsletter

Submission Guidelines

Spread the news, get the word out, communicate, share
Send us your news and events!

Designed to inform staff of upcoming events, activities, and recent news, within the Division of Student Affairs, the newsletter is distributed each month to all Student Life staff via email.

Student Affairs staff are invited to send your upcoming events and noteworthy items to Kerri A. Kadow, Communications Manager, at k-kadow@neiu.edu. Please follow the submission guidelines below:

When to send items: Anytime. We will send out email reminders when the deadlines draw near.

Where to send items: Via email attachments to Kerri A. Kadow (k-kadow@neiu.edu)

Format:

  • Send as an attachment in MS Word
  • Each item should be one paragraph (approximately 150 words or less).
  • Be sure to include relevant information such as:
    •  dates,
    • times,
    • location of event,
    • who to contact for more information (name and phone number/email.)
  • Dates, times, and location should be in the event title/header.
  • Please follow the format in the example.

What to send:

Information about:

  • Upcoming events on campus
  • Small or large scale programs (i.e., Orientation programs, Job Fairs, Special Programming weeks/months)
  • Other activities that your office may want to highlight
  • Professional accomplishments of staff in your area
  • Professional development and training programs of interest to staff in Student Affairs.

You may also include an image (photo or clipart) you would like included along with the item. Images are included at the discretion of the editor.

I recommend copying your supervisor on the email (if applicable) so they are aware of what you sent to me.

More information:

The Student Affairs E-Newsletter will typically be a monthly publication with a few exceptions (i.e., holiday breaks), and is sent to all Student Affairs staff and other key staff at NEIU. The deadline will usually be a week prior to the date of each issue.

If too few items are received for any particular issue, the issue may be cancelled (although the significance of the events/items received will be taken into consideration), and items may be included in a subsequent issue if timely.

As always, feel free to email Kerri at k-kadow@neiu.edu if you have questions or need clarification. I look forward to receiving your submissions!
