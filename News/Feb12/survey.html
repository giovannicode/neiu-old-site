Internal Communications Survey Results

Many thanks to all who responded to the Division of Student Life internal communications survey conducted last fall. The information provided will be used to shape the methods we will use to reach out and communicate with staff in Student Affairs and the types of information on which we will focus. Given the 52.9% response rate (45 responses out of the 85 staff to which the survey was sent) it’s clear many of you made time to respond. Below is a brief summary of the findings.

In response to the question on the type of information staff would like to see included in internal communications, there were three clear preferences. The top choice with an average rating of 4.52 was information on the programs and events sponsored by offices in the Division, followed by staff wanting information on professional development programs sponsored by Student Life (4.49 average rating) and off-campus professional development opportunities (4.33 average rating). Given that feedback, it’s been agreed that the focus of future internal communications will be on informing staff of the programs, services, and events sponsored by offices within Student Affairs and on professional development opportunities both on and off campus.

In contrast, when asked about the type of communications staff viewed as helping them stay informed, there was a wider range of responses, with slightly higher ratings being given to 1) listservs for staff based on areas of interest (i.e., advising, assessment, etc.) (3.93 average rating), 2) Internal Newsletter distributed via e-mail (3.84 average rating), and regular e-mails from the Vice President (3.79 average rating).  Given these responses, it’s been decided that we will revive the internal e-newsletter that was sent out to staff in Student Life, while Vice President Ross will continue to send periodic e-mails out to staff.  The topic of how to best approach listservs will be discussed with the Professional Development Committee.

Feel free to contact Kris Pierre at k-pierre@neiu.edu if you would like more specifics on the results from the Internal Communications survey.
