New Targeted Announcements Procedures

Recently, it was announced that we have a new set of Targeted Announcement Procedures and a Targeted Announcement Form that should be used by all offices and programs within the Division of Student Affairs. Highlighted below are some minor modifications that have been made to those guidelines in response to feedback we received last fall:

  1. In an effort to streamline the process and insure a faster turnaround, Directors/Unit Heads will be the ones responsible for doing a thorough review of content to insure it is correct before being sent sending on for posting. Therefore, requests to post targeted announcements should be e-mailed by the Director or Unit Head to Kerri Kadow (k-kadow@neiu.edu) and Jim Ryan (j-ryan2@neiu.edu). If that individual is away from campus, the Assistant or Associate Director or another individual who has been designated to serve in the absence of the department head may send the e-mail request. There is no longer a need to copy Associate Vice President Daniel Lopez on these requests.


  2. Jim Ryan in the Counseling Office has kindly agreed to serve as a back up to Kerri Kadow for posting announcements. Given that, it is important that you include both he and Kerri on requests for targeted announcements (to insure that Jim gets the information in the event Kerri is out of the office). Kerri or Jim will proofread announcements and edit for grammar, appearance, and style. If major changes are needed, the revised announcement will be sent back for review by the Unit Head before posting.


  3. To insure faster posting of your announcement offices are encouraged to fill out the form and to follow the guidelines in the NEIU Identity Manual which may be found at: http://www.neiu.edu/omwc/brand/University_Identity_Manual.html (a summary can also be found at http://www.neiu.edu/~stulife/PublicationsGuidelines.pdf).

The complete Targeted Announcement procedures and form can be found at the following links: Procedures / Form.  If you have any questions on these procedures please don’t hesitate to contact Kerri Kadow or Kris Pierre.
